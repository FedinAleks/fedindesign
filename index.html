How I Work

1

Briefing

Briefing helps understand the project requirements and create a concept that meets its needs. The success of the project depends on this stage, so it is important to devote proper attention and time to it. This process facilitates understanding of the purpose, target audience, and key functionalities that the project should fulfill.

2

Analysis and Prototyping

Analysis and Prototyping are crucial stages in the web design process. Analysis involves gathering and assessing information about the project, including client requirements, target audience, competitors, and market trends. This phase helps identify key objectives and establish a solid foundation for the project. Prototyping, on the other hand, involves creating a preliminary version of the website to visualize its layout, functionality, and user experience. It allows for early testing and feedback, enabling designers to refine and improve the design before full development. These stages are essential for ensuring that the final product meets the client's needs and achieves its goals effectively.

3

Graphic Design Development

The process of developing the visual part involves creating a design that reflects the style and brand identity, selecting colors and fonts, designing interface elements for a convenient user experience, and adapting to different devices. This stage includes creating layouts, working on design elements, and testing for aesthetic appeal and usability. Additionally, feedback is collected for further refinement of the design to meet the needs of clients and users.

4

Handover of the Finished Project

The final stage involves delivering the finalized layout to the client or development team. Typically, this includes providing all necessary design files. Clear documentation or guidance may also be provided to ensure successful implementation of the design. Additionally, the designer may offer support or consultations during the development phase to address any questions or issues that arise. This stage marks the transition from design to development, where the visual concept comes to life on websites or other digital platforms.
